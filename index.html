Dear Shani Francis,

During our Conversation, you told me how you started out as a vet assistant, then stepped into teaching temporarily… and how that temporary role slowly


became something more permanent. At first it sounded like such an odd — vet to teacher — two completely different worlds.


But the more I thought about it, the more it made sense.


Both roles take the same kind of heart.


A heart that wants to heal.


A heart that wants to help others grow.


A heart that steps in when someone or something needs care, patience, and stability.


That kind of heart is rare, and it says a lot about who you are.


You exemplify:


Galatians 6:2


“Carry each other’s burdens, and in this way you will fulfill the law of Christ.”


I want to impress on you that this is a gift from God. Not everyone is wired this way. Not everyone has compassion strong enough to cross careers and still


serve with the same purpose.


You carry something good, something meaningful, and something the world needs.


Remember always:


1 Peter 4:10


“Each of you should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful stewards of God’s grace in its various forms.”


–Tola


P.S I think you corrected the way Zane said your name, which was how I had assumed it was pronounced too. Then I realized it sounded Jewish/Hebrew,


now with context which made sense, so I asked. I like names, so I looked into it.


In Jewish / Hebrew context:


• Shani (שָׁנִי) is a modern Hebrew girl’s name that literally means “crimson / scarlet” — a deep, rich red.


• The word shani is used in the Old Testament for scarlet thread and scarlet fabric, a color tied to richness, importance, and sacrifice.


So in Jewish/Hebrew roots, your name carries the idea of a deep scarlet color.


In Jamaican / Caribbean context:


Jamaicans use the name Shani mainly through wider Afro-Caribbean/African influence, not from a separate Jamaican-language meaning.


• In African/Swahili and African-Caribbean usage, Shani commonly means “marvelous” or “wonderful.”

(There is also a chance that it comes from jewish roots for them as well.)


Francis — meaning


• Francis comes from the Latin Franciscus, meaning “free one” or “one who is free.”


• It also carries the sense of “belonging to the Franks,” an ancient people whose name is tied to freedom.


• In Christian history, the name is closely associated with Saint Francis, known for compassion, humility, and care for the vulnerable.


So when you put it all together, Shani Francis carries the blended meaning of:


“A marvelous, scarlet-colored thread of significance… one who walks in freedom.”
